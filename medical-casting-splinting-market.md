<p><p>The medical casting and splinting market primarily consists of two main types: casting and splinting. Casting involves the application of rigid materials, such as plaster or fiberglass, to immobilize broken bones or support injured limbs, ensuring proper healing. Splinting, on the other hand, utilizes flexible or semi-rigid materials to stabilize and protect an injured area without full immobilization, allowing for some movement. Both methods are essential in orthopedic care, addressing various types of fractures and soft tissue injuries.</p></p>
