<p><p>The car phone market primarily operates on two network types: CDMA and GSM. CDMA (Code Division Multiple Access) networks use unique codes to differentiate calls, offering efficient spectrum usage and strong call quality, often found in North America. GSM (Global System for Mobile Communications) networks use SIM cards for user identification, enabling easy device switching and global compatibility, prevalent in Europe and Asia. Both technologies support car phones, with CDMA focusing on robust performance and GSM emphasizing flexibility and international use.</p></p>
