<p><p>Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave Radar (FMCW Radar) is a type of radar system that uses a continuous wave signal with frequency modulation to measure the distance and speed of objects. <p><ul><li>Sawtooth Modulation</li><li>Triangular Modulation</li><li>Square-wave Modulation</li><li>Stepped Modulation</li><li>Sinusoidal Modulation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave (FMCW) Radar is a technology that emits continuous radio waves with varying frequencies to measure distance and speed of targets. The market types include Sawtooth Modulation, which ramps frequency linearly over time; Triangular Modulation, featuring a linear rise and fall; and Square-wave Modulation, generating sharp frequency changes. Stepped Modulation involves discrete frequency changes, while Sinusoidal Modulation uses smooth, cyclical changes. Each modulation type serves different applications, affecting performance in range resolution and target detection capabilities.</p></p>
