<p><p>The bladder cancer treatment drugs market is segmented into two primary types: non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC) and muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC). NMIBC treatments focus on localized tumors that have not penetrated the bladder wall, often involving immunotherapy and intravesical therapies. In contrast, MIBC treatments are aimed at more aggressive forms of the disease that invade deeper tissues, typically requiring a combination of chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and surgical interventions. Each segment reflects the distinct therapeutic approaches tailored to specific cancer stages.</p></p>
