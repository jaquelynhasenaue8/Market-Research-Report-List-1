<p><p>The conjugate vaccine market is categorized into monovalent and multivalent vaccines. Monovalent conjugate vaccines target a single pathogen, enhancing immunity against specific infections, like those caused by certain strains of bacteria. In contrast, multivalent conjugate vaccines provide protection against multiple pathogens or strains within one vaccine, broadening coverage and improving public health outcomes. Both types play crucial roles in preventing diseases, particularly in vulnerable populations, and are essential tools in global vaccination strategies.</p></p>
