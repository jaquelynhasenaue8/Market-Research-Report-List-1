<p><p>Nucleic acid-based therapeutics encompass a variety of innovative treatments targeting genetic material. Anti-sense and anti-gene therapies inhibit gene expression by binding to specific RNA or DNA sequences. Short inhibitory sequences (siRNAs) silence genes to prevent disease progression. Gene transfer therapy introduces genetic material into cells to correct deficiencies. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleotides, disrupting cell replication. Ribozymes are RNA molecules that catalyze reactions, while aptamers are synthetic oligonucleotides that bind selectively to target proteins, offering diverse therapeutic possibilities.</p></p>
