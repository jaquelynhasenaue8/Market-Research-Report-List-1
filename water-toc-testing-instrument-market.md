<p><p>The Water Total Organic Carbon (TOC) Testing Instrument market includes several methods for measuring organic carbon levels in water. The NDIR (Non-Dispersive Infrared) method utilizes infrared light to detect carbon dioxide produced from organic compounds. The UV method employs ultraviolet light to oxidize organic materials, allowing measurement of carbon levels. Conductance methods gauge the electrical conductivity changes in water, which are correlated to organic carbon content. Other methods may include advanced oxidation processes or chromatographic techniques, expanding the testing capabilities.</p></p>
