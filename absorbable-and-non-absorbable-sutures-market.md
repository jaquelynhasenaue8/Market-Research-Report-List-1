<p><p>Absorbable and non-absorbable sutures are two primary categories of surgical sutures with distinct characteristics and applications. Absorbable sutures are made from materials that are gradually broken down by the body, eliminating the need for removal. They are commonly used in internal tissues, where healing occurs over a few weeks. Non-absorbable sutures, on the other hand, are made from materials that do not degrade in the body, providing long-term support and requiring eventual removal. These sutures are often utilized in external applications or in environments where prolonged stability is essential.</p><p>The Absorbable and Non-Absorbable Sutures Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.3% during the forecast period, driven by increasing surgical procedures worldwide, advancements in suture materials, and rising demand for minimally invasive surgeries. Additionally, a growing focus on patient safety and the need for faster recovery times are further propelling market growth. Innovations and expanding applications across various surgeries, including orthopedic, cardiovascular, and cosmetic procedures, are expected to sustain the momentum in this market segment.</p></p>
