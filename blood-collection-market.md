EDTA tubes are used for preserving blood samples for hematology tests, preventing coagulation. Plasma separation tubes facilitate the separation of plasma for various analyses. Other categories may include specialized tubes for DNA, microbiology, or drug testing, catering to diverse diagnostic requirements within clinical laboratories and research settings.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection market is vital for clinical diagnostics and research, encompassing two primary methods: venous and capillary blood collection. Venous blood collection involves drawing blood from a vein, typically using a needle and syringe, which provides larger sample volumes for various tests. In contrast, capillary blood collection utilizes a fingerstick or heel puncture, ideal for quick tests and point-of-care applications, allowing for rapid result delivery. Both methods are essential for monitoring health and diagnosing conditions effectively.</p></p>
